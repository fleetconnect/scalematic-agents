### Q-004 — "Aaron's Brain" (Instantly Co-Pilot ruleset) — APPROVED 2026-06-11 (with attribution + the 4 deltas below)
**Origin:** third-party (Aaron Reid) cold-email operating rules + master prompt, 10 rules.
**Assessment:** Philosophically the *most aligned* import in the system — value-first/gift mindset, no pitch in first touch, explicit "Zero Scarcity, Zero Hype" rule, no vanity personalization, internal-note tone, binary low-friction CTAs, Poke-the-Bear (Josh Braun, attributed). Passes the Judgment Gate at the philosophy level. This is effectively the missing **email-channel encoding** of the ScaleMatic conversation philosophy.
**Deltas to reconcile before APPROVED status:**
1. **Personalization encoding conflict (resolved as channel rule):** Aaron's Brain bans explicit observation ("I saw your post"); ScaleMatic LinkedIn practice *requires* explicit observed evidence. Both serve one judgment — personalization must be proven by work, not claimed. Canonical resolution → CR-1 note: **email = implied personalization (the insight proves the work); LinkedIn DM = explicit observation (the citation proves the work).** Neither encoding crosses channels.
2. **Its numbers enter the Constraint Registry** as the Instantly-channel canon (40–60 word first touch, 60–90 follow-up, ≤4-word subject, ≤7-word binary CTA, 2-touch / 3–5 day sequence) — see CR-1/CR-2 amendments.
3. **Gift deliverability is a truth-condition:** every promised asset ("should I send it?") must be genuinely deliverable fast, or the gift becomes the lie — JR-002 applies to offers, not just scarcity. Aaron's Brain's own "effortless to deliver" test covers this; make it a hard gate.
4. Minor: the master prompt's "IGNORE ALL PREVIOUS INSTRUCTIONS" header and "do not ask follow-up questions" are compensation-era artifacts (and a governance smell in a multi-agent system). Strip when porting into the OS; keep the ruleset.

## REJECTED (retired with reasons — all rejections R-001–R-008 confirmed by founder 2026-06-11)

### R-001 — "Every human is a buyer. If they don't buy from you, you haven't made the right offer at the right time."
**From:** conversion-dms core principle.
**Why rejected:** Directly contradicts JR-012 (disqualification is a feature) and JR-001 (reputation-adjusted revenue). A worldview in which every non-sale is a seller failure produces exactly the forcing behavior the entire OS exists to prevent. The sentence is also empirically false for a qualified-fit business model.
**What it cost while live:** philosophy collision risk in any agent routing to conversion-dms for DM tasks; potential brand damage via template execution.

### R-002 — Walkthrough-template cold openers (all variants).
**Why rejected:** Pattern-match to mass DM spam; auto-fail governance L1 (authenticity) and L4 (specificity). The recipient-side experience is indistinguishable from the volume automation the brand publicly condemns.

### R-003 — "Cold-to-close 10–40%" and adjacent imported benchmarks.
**Why rejected:** Implausible as cold outbound economics; unverifiable provenance; an unverified number stated confidently inside an OS corrupts downstream planning. CR-4 governs all benchmarks now.

### R-004 — Manufactured scarcity without truth-condition.
**Why rejected:** Violated governance L3 while living inside an approved skill (strip-line) — the audit's executable-contradiction finding. Replaced by JR-002's conditional form, not banned outright: real constraints may be stated.

---

### R-005 — The grief-conversion play (Setter Bible, "Prospect dealing with grief / loss / death").
**Why rejected:** The script converts a disclosed bereavement into a framework pitch and a booked call inside three messages. This is the single clearest JR-001 violation in any source document — reputation-terminal if ever screenshotted, and wrong regardless. **Note:** the acknowledgment table from the same document teaches handling disclosure with genuine care ("I appreciate you sharing that") — keep the acknowledgment craft, reject its weaponization. When a prospect discloses grief: acknowledge, give space, park the thread. No pivot. Ever.

### R-006 — Pretext openers and bait-and-switch as doctrine.
**From:** Setter Bible "market research" openers + training archive (5.15.23: "Bait and switch explanation — sell them what they want, give them what they need").
**Why rejected:** The pretext frame — opening under a stated purpose that isn't the real purpose — fails governance L5 (screenshot test) and JR-001 the moment it's recognized, and the setter industry has made it widely recognized. Bait-and-switch is not a tactic with bad optics; it's the philosophical opposite of JR-004. **Truth-condition for survival:** "research" framing is permissible only when the research is real and its findings are delivered regardless of commercial outcome (which is the Aaron's Brain gift model — the honest descendant of this tactic).

### R-007 — Pressure tie-downs and guilt-based follow-up.
**From:** Setter Bible: 1–10 commitment scale + "why not a 10?" escalation · "PROMISE me you'll let me know" · closer-scarcity framing ("he's usually booked solid… might be able to squeeze you in") · 20-minute "?" bumps, "You alive? lol," ghost GIFs.
**Why rejected:** Each manufactures compliance, urgency, or guilt — direct violations of JR-002, CR-5 prohibitions, and the deghosting protocol's no-guilt rule. The 20-minute bump cadence also contradicts CR-2's 72-hour spacing by two orders of magnitude. These are the precise patterns the governance layer's auto-block list was built against; this document is where they came from.

### R-008 — Volume-quota call theater.
**From:** Setter Bible goal logic: "Set the 5 BEST calls you can… sometimes the best is 5 [unqualified bookings] (we will cancel them)."
**Why rejected:** Booking known-bad calls to satisfy a daily quota, then canceling them, is metric gaming that optimizes the dashboard against the business — and the casual dehumanization in the source text is itself the reputation hazard JR-001 exists to prevent. ScaleMatic's stated KPI philosophy (qualified conversations, not activity counts) supersedes quota logic wholesale. A day with two real conversations beats a day with five theatrical bookings.



Nothing enters APPROVED without: (1) source attribution, (2) a pass through the Judgment Gate's five questions applied to the framework's *philosophy*, not just its tactics, (3) template-level governance review, (4) benchmark provenance tagging, (5) founder sign-off. The conversion-dms lesson, stated once: **a framework that works is not the same as a framework that's yours.**
