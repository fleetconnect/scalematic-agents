# Paid Ads — Meta Low-Budget Lead Gen

## Pillar Overview
Meta ads (Facebook + Instagram) at low budget ($15–$50/day) for service-based businesses, coaches, and consultants. The goal is not brand awareness — it is generating qualified conversations and leads that convert to sales calls or DM conversations.

This is a performance frame, not a branding frame.

---

## Low-Budget Meta Framework

### Phase 1: Message-First Creative
Before spending on ads, you need to know what message converts organically. The best ad creative is usually a direct lift from a DM, comment, or post that already got a response. Test organic first, then amplify with budget.

### Phase 2: Offer Clarity
Ads amplify what already works. If your offer isn't converting in DMs, ads will burn budget. The ad's job is to get a click or a message — the offer's job is to convert.

Ad types that work at low budget:
- Direct response text-based image ads (dark background, single bold claim)
- Short-form video ads under 30 seconds with a problem-first hook
- Lead magnet ads driving to a Messenger or DM conversation
- Retargeting ads to organic video viewers (cheapest warm traffic)

### Phase 3: Targeting Philosophy
At low budget, broad is often better than hyper-targeted. Let Meta's algorithm find buyers. Narrow targeting with small audiences leads to frequency spikes and creative fatigue fast.

Audiences that work:
- Lookalikes of past buyers (requires at least 100 buyer records)
- Broad interest targeting with strong creative doing the qualifying
- Retargeting: page engagers, video viewers (3s+), website visitors

### Phase 4: Funnel for Low-Budget Lead Gen
Entry: Ad → Lead magnet or direct message CTA
Middle: DM conversation or email nurture sequence
Conversion: Offer presentation in DM or short call

At $15–$30/day, expect 3–10 leads/day depending on niche and creative quality.

---

## Creative Principles for Service Businesses
- Lead with the problem, not the solution
- Use "you" more than "I"
- Make the cost of inaction feel real
- Avoid stock photos — real faces, real environments outperform
- One CTA per ad — not "click, comment, and DM us"

---

## What Kills Low-Budget Campaigns
- Creative that talks about features instead of outcomes
- Sending ad traffic to a cold website instead of a conversation
- Running the same creative for more than 2 weeks without testing new hooks
- Targeting too narrow with too small a budget
- Not retargeting warm audiences first
