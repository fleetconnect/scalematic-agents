# Outbound DM Frameworks

## Pillar Overview
Conversion through direct conversation — no sales calls required in the early stages. The goal is to qualify, build micro-trust, and move prospects toward a clear next step inside the chat.

---

## Hook-Starter Method
A cold DM that leads with a hook specific enough to feel personal but sharp enough to trigger a pattern interrupt.

Structure:
1. **Observation hook** — reference something specific about their content, company, or situation (never generic compliments)
2. **Insight drop** — share a single non-obvious truth relevant to their problem
3. **Soft pivot** — ask one clarifying question that opens a real conversation

Rules:
- Never pitch in message 1
- The hook must feel like it was written specifically for them
- Length: under 5 lines
- No "I came across your profile" openers

Example angle: "Saw your post about [X]. Most people in your position [common mistake]. The ones who fix it first usually [outcome]."

---

## Dallas Method
Named after the framework for closing high-intent conversations without a call.

Phases:
1. **Diagnose** — ask questions to surface the exact problem and current state
2. **Amplify** — reflect the cost of the problem back to them (lost revenue, time, opportunity)
3. **Leverage** — introduce your solution only after they've articulated the pain
4. **Lock in** — propose a clear, low-friction next step (not a call — a yes/no decision)
5. **Affirm** — confirm commitment and give them a specific action

Key insight: most closers jump to leverage before the prospect has felt the cost. The Dallas Method slows down phases 1-2 to do the selling for you.

---

## Conversion Without Sales Calls
The belief: a sales call is not always the optimal next step. Many low-ticket to mid-ticket buyers want to buy before they talk.

Framework for no-call conversion:
1. Qualify via DM (2-3 targeted questions about situation + urgency)
2. Micro-pitch via voice note or video loom (under 90 seconds, personalized)
3. Direct to low-friction buying action (checkout, brief form, or quick onboarding call under 15 min)

When to use calls: high-ticket ($3k+), complex implementations, or when the prospect signals confusion
