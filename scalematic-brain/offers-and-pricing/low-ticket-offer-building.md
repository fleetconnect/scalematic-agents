# Low-Ticket Offer Building

## Pillar Overview
Low-ticket offers ($27–$297) are engineered to do three things: generate qualified buyers at near-zero CAC, prove demand for a core methodology, and create a self-liquidating acquisition path. They are not "cheap versions" of a high-ticket offer — they are a distinct product designed for a specific transformation.

---

## Anatomy of a Strong Low-Ticket Offer

### 1. Single Outcome Focus
A low-ticket offer solves one specific, urgent problem. Not a "full system." Not "everything you need." One win, clearly described, achievable in a defined timeframe.

Bad: "Build your outbound system"
Good: "Write 5 DMs that get replies — in one afternoon"

### 2. Urgency Through Specificity
The offer title and hook must contain a time frame, a number, or a specific mechanism. Vague offers don't sell at low ticket.

Formula: [Outcome] + [Timeframe or Constraint] + [Mechanism/Method]
Example: "Close 3 clients this week using nothing but LinkedIn DMs — no ads, no calls"

### 3. Mechanism Naming
Name the method. "The Dallas Method," "The Hook-Starter Framework," "The 3-Step Qualifier." Named mechanisms signal that this is systematic and not just advice.

### 4. Implicit Upsell Architecture
Every low-ticket product should leave the buyer wanting one thing: help implementing what they just learned. The gap between knowing and doing is where the high-ticket offer lives.

## Offer Formats That Work at Low Ticket
- PDF/Workbook ($27–$47): fast wins, high perceived value if well-designed
- Workshop recording ($47–$97): good for frameworks with demonstration
- Mini-course ($97–$197): 3-5 short modules, one skill or system
- Template bundle ($47–$97): plug-and-play assets (scripts, sequences, trackers)
- Live workshop ($97–$297): urgency, community, real-time Q&A

---

## Pricing Psychology
- $27: impulse buy, no consideration required
- $47–$97: still low friction, buyers feel like they're getting value
- $97–$197: begins to feel like a commitment — requires stronger proof/outcome clarity
- $197–$297: bridge between low and mid ticket — needs a clear ROI story

---

## What Makes Low-Ticket Offers Fail
- Trying to pack in too much (becomes overwhelming, not actionable)
- No clear outcome promise on the sales page
- Generic mechanisms ("strategies and tactics")
- No natural next step toward a higher-priced offer
- Priced too low to attract serious buyers ($7–$17 attracts freebie seekers)
